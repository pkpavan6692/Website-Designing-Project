About Web Development

Web development is the way toward building websites and applications for the web or a private network known as an intranet. Website development is not concerned about the design and layout of a website; instead, it's focused on the coding and programming that controls the website's functionality. From the most basic, static site pages to social media platforms and applications, from e-commerce sites to content management system (CMS); web developers have developed every tool we use via the internet.

In the world we live in today, rather than being a trend, a website is a necessity. No matter how small or how big an organization is, to survive and succeed in this era of digitization, making a website and reaching out to the crowd using online methods are necessary. Tim Berners-Lee developed the concept of the World Wide Web in 1989. He was a scientist at CERN, the European Organization for Nuclear Research. This idea gave birth to Hypertext Markup Language (HTML) in 1990. In the beginning, HTML was text-focused and there was limited scope for image, content positioning, and formatting. However, based on SGML (Standard Generalized Markup Language), HTML became a fundamental part of a website. Due to SGML, it became effortless for coders to design webpage layouts, which were interactive and easy to use.
